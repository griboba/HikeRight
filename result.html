            Offline Survival Guides

            • Bleeding control (Red Cross): apply firm direct pressure with a clean cloth; do not remove it. If available, use a tourniquet on limbs 2–3 in above the wound.
            • Shock: lay the person flat, elevate legs 12 in (unless spinal injury suspected), keep warm, do not give food or water.
            • Hypothermia: move to shelter, remove wet clothing, insulate from ground, add heat packs to armpits and groin. Monitor breathing.
            • Signal for help (NPS): whistle 3 blasts, pause, repeat. Mirror flashes in open sun. Create large ground-to-air symbols (V = help needed, X = medical, → = traveling this direction).
            • If lost — STOP (NPS): Stop. Think. Observe. Plan. Stay put if dark or severe weather is near; rescuers find stationary people faster.
            • Kids — Hug-a-Tree (NPS): tell children to hug a tree when lost and use their whistle every minute so searchers can find them.

            Guidelines adapted from American Red Cross First Aid ↗  &  NPS Emergency Safety ↗
